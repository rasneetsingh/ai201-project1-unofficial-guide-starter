QUESTION (OP):
I'm about to graduate with my PhD in September and feeling the pressure of job hunting. As an F1 student, I need to find a job within three months of defending to maintain my visa status. I've applied to at least 50 jobs this year — consulting roles and big pharma companies like Merck, NEB, and Amgen — but it's been mostly silence or rejections. I've considered breaking into life science consulting: is it possible to take a consulting job on OPT? Any advice on the application process, or general job search strategy (networking, tailoring resumes, applying through recruiters)?
 
COMMENTS / ADVICE:
 
The US job market is rough right now, and finding a job that will also sponsor you is very difficult — especially in desirable fields like consulting and pharma.
 
This reflects the current climate. The job market is hard, and even more so if you require sponsorship. The best move is to keep applying, and to curate your resume and cover letter to each individual job rather than sending the same one everywhere.
 
Consider also applying to jobs in your home country as a backup, just to be safe.
 
Applying to 50 jobs is not enough in the current market — you need to apply to many more.
 
OPT unemployment workaround (volunteering): If you can't find a paid job within the allowed window, a volunteer position can help you maintain status and extend your stay. You generally need to volunteer around 20 hours per week and get a letter from the organization documenting your volunteer appointment, which you then file with your international student office. A lab on campus is one possible option. This lets you stay in status part-time while continuing to job hunt.
 
Mass applying isn't effective on its own. Work on tailoring your resume to each posting, actually read the job descriptions carefully, and network aggressively.
