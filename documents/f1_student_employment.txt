QUESTION (OP):
What are F1 students allowed and not allowed to do regarding part-time work? I heard you can work 20 hours a week but only on campus — how does it work during summer?
 
COMMENTS / ADVICE:
 
During summer, you can work up to 40 hours per week on campus without CPT, provided your university allows it. You can work off-campus part-time or full-time with CPT authorization, and the off-campus job must align with what you're studying.
 
More detail on hour limits: When school is not in session (summer, fall break, spring break), you're allowed to work full-time, up to 40 hours/week total across all jobs — one full-time job or multiple part-time jobs, as long as the combined total doesn't exceed 40 hours. When classes are back in session, you cannot work more than 20 hours/week total (again, across all jobs combined). This applies to on-campus employment, which does not require a work permit. Off-campus employment follows the same hour logic but requires federal work authorization (e.g., through OPT/CPT).
 
Important warning on OPT vs. CPT during summer: Do NOT use OPT during the summer while you're still in school — pre-completion OPT used during the summer eats into the OPT time you'd have after graduation. Summer off-campus work should be done under CPT and must be related to your major.
 
On whether jobs must be major-related: As an international student, essentially all of your authorized off-campus work (OPT or CPT) must be related to your major — always. You generally cannot take an unrelated job (e.g., a random non-major summer job just to make money) under CPT. (On-campus jobs are the more flexible exception.)
 
On CPT affecting OPT: One commenter worried that going the off-campus CPT route during summer could affect post-completion OPT. The clarification: using CPT only affects OPT eligibility if you work 12 months (365+ days) of full-time CPT continuously. Working only for the duration of a summer does not affect your later OPT.
 
Short version from another commenter: "You can work full time in summer."
